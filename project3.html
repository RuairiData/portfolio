Project 3 — Tik Box (Habit Analytics)

Problem: Users needed a simple way to track habits, understand progress over time, and identify patterns that explain success/failure (e.g., which days are missed, what habits “cluster” together).

BI framing (what makes this relevant to Power BI work): This is a KPI reporting and insights product. It mirrors what most BI teams build—standardised metrics, time-based trends, target tracking, and drill-down analysis—only the “stakeholders” are app users rather than underwriters/ops leads.

Core metrics (Power BI equivalents):

  • Completion rate (days marked / days available)
  • Weekly goal attainment (completions this week vs goal per week)
  • Cumulative progress (running total by week/year-to-date)
  • Day-of-week insight (best day / most skipped day)
  • Pairing insight (habits most frequently completed together)

Data model (how I would structure it in Power BI):

  • FactCompletions: date, habit_id, completed (1/0)
  • DimHabits: habit, weekly_goal, category/colour
  • DimDate: standard calendar table for time intelligence

Outcome: A clean “overview → drill-down” journey (insights page to habit detail), with metrics that are consistent, explainable, and easy to act on—exactly the qualities hiring managers want in MI/Power BI reporting.
